# Branding & logo

## Name

**“DiscoBall” is a working codename and may change before launch.** It’s a
playful nod to the product’s two core ideas — a *disco ball* (many small facets
reflecting one bright whole) and a *calendar* (squares, days, planning). If a
different name is chosen, update: `README.md`, `package.json` (`name`), the app
title in `src/app/layout.tsx`, the org/app references in docs, and the logo.

Name directions worth considering if “DiscoBall” doesn’t stick: *Facet*,
*Roster*, *Ledger*, *Cadence*, *Matterboard*, *Tessera* (a mosaic tile — ties to
the facet/calendar-square motif).

## Logo concept

The intended mark **fuses a disco ball with a desk calendar**. Two concrete
directions:

1. **Mirror-ball of calendar squares.** A sphere whose facets are tiny calendar
   cells — a few cells highlighted (like a few mirrors catching light) to read
   as “today / busy days.” The sphere silhouette says *disco ball*; the gridded
   facets say *calendar/planning*.
2. **Calendar grid wrapping a sphere.** A flat month grid that curves around a
   ball, with one date square popped forward. Cleaner, more “app icon” friendly.

**Palette:** a violet primary (`--primary: #6d28d9`, already used in the UI)
with a light/dark neutral surround; optional subtle prismatic accent on a couple
of facets to evoke reflected light without being noisy.

**Usage:** a square app/icon lockup plus a horizontal wordmark
(`🪩 DiscoBall`). Keep it legible at 16–24 px (favicon / sidebar).
